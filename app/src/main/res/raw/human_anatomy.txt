Human Anatomy

Human anatomy is the study of the structure of the human body.

Major Systems:
1. Skeletal System:
   - Provides structure and support.
   - Protects organs and facilitates movement.

2. Muscular System:
   - Enables movement.
   - Maintains posture and generates heat.

3. Circulatory System:
   - Heart, blood, and vessels transport nutrients and oxygen.

4. Respiratory System:
   - Lungs and airways for gas exchange (O2 in, CO2 out).

5. Digestive System:
   - Breaks down food, absorbs nutrients, eliminates waste.

6. Nervous System:
   - Brain, spinal cord, nerves.
   - Controls body functions and responds to stimuli.

7. Endocrine System:
   - Glands producing hormones to regulate processes.

8. Reproductive System:
   - Ensures continuation of species.

9. Urinary System:
   - Filters blood and removes waste as urine.

10. Immune System:
    - Protects against pathogens and diseases.
