Zoology

Zoology is the study of animals, their structure, function, behavior, and evolution.

Key Areas:
1. Animal Classification:
   - Vertebrates and invertebrates.
   - Taxonomy and evolutionary history.

2. Anatomy and Physiology:
   - Organ systems and body structures.
   - Adaptations for survival.

3. Animal Behavior:
   - Communication, reproduction, migration.
   - Learned vs instinctive behaviors.

4. Reproductive Biology:
   - Life cycles, fertilization, development.
   - Reproductive strategies.

5. Evolution and Diversity:
   - Natural selection and speciation.
   - Diversity among major animal groups.

6. Ecological Roles:
   - Food chains, predator-prey relationships.
   - Importance in ecosystems.

Applications:
- Wildlife conservation, ecology, veterinary science.
